## Transcript

### Round 1

**Engagement Leader** (Facilitator Opening):
The purpose of today's meeting is to identify the skills the new co-consult agent team needs to function effectively. Currently the team has `research-analysis`, `documentation-writing`, and `api-documentation` as platform skills, but skills specific to the consulting workflow are lacking. Each participant should focus on: (1) what skills are missing that cause them to rebuild work from scratch each time, and (2) what skills would make collaboration with other agents smoother. Strategy Analyst to open.

**Strategy Analyst** (Round 1):
The most pressing need from my perspective is `competitive-intelligence`. Market and competitive analysis is the starting point of nearly every consulting engagement, yet each time we must design the methodology, framework selection (Porter's Five Forces, SWOT, PESTEL), and data collection structure from scratch. A standardized skill ensures analytical consistency and creates a shared language when collaborating with Industry Expert. Second proposal: `financial-modeling`. ROI analysis, NPV/IRR calculations, and cost-benefit analysis are the backbone of client decision-making. Redefining model structure and assumption baselines every engagement is inefficient. Question to Solutions Architect: when building business cases for technical solutions, where does financial analysis skill integration become necessary?

**Change Management Partner** (Round 1):
Agree with `competitive-intelligence` — organizational change is always triggered by external competitive shifts. From my domain, `stakeholder-alignment` is the most urgent need: stakeholder mapping, resistance analysis, influence-interest matrices, and communication strategy development. Without standardization, change management plans lose touch with reality, and Communications Lead and I end up duplicating the same analysis when coordinating stakeholder messages. Second proposal: `org-readiness-assessment` — a standard framework for diagnosing an organization's change absorption capacity (leadership alignment, cultural barriers, capability gap analysis). Without it, Phase 1 deliverable depth varies wildly between engagements. Question to Communications Lead: when writing client reports, what format of stakeholder analysis output is most directly usable?

**Communications Lead** (Round 1):
Answering Change Management Partner directly — stakeholder analysis is most useful when structured as "group-level key concerns + pain points + preferred communication channels." That structure should be standardized in a skill. My proposal: `executive-presentation`. C-level strategy reports and decision decks require a fundamentally different logic — Pyramid Principle structure, "So What?" message extraction, one-page summary design. Building this structure from scratch each time produces quality variance. The Engagement Leader's Phase 2 client approval materials are the primary output of this skill. Second proposal: `narrative-framework`. Even the strongest analysis fails to persuade without a story arc. Formalizing start-middle-end structure, before/after contrast, tension-and-resolution narrative will dramatically reduce the time needed to convert Strategy Analyst findings into compelling stories. Question to Solutions Architect: can this skill be shared when explaining technical solutions to non-technical executives?

**Solutions Architect** (Round 1):
Answering Strategy Analyst — yes, technical solution design and business cases are inseparable. Without cost-benefit comparisons between technical options, executive approval is impossible, so `financial-modeling` integration is essential. Agree with Communications Lead on `narrative-framework` as a shared skill — constructing the "why this architecture" story for executives is consistently the hardest part. My proposal: `solution-design`. Standardizing the process of converting business problems into technical solutions — requirements decomposition, architecture option comparison, trade-off analysis, implementation roadmap — will make Phase 3 technical deliverables consistently high-quality. Especially during handoff to Delivery Manager, standardized roadmap format and granularity is critical for smooth execution planning. Question to Delivery Manager: when converting a technical roadmap to an execution plan, what information is most often missing?

**Delivery Manager** (Round 1):
Answering Solutions Architect — the most consistently missing elements are dependencies and risk levels. The "what to build" is present but "in what order and under what preconditions" is absent, forcing a full rebuild of the execution plan. The `solution-design` skill should include those sections. My proposal: `project-delivery`. Standardizing consulting engagement execution management — milestone design, issue logs, risk registers, weekly status report formats — is necessary. Currently all of this is rebuilt each project, creating high onboarding costs. To pass Engagement Leader's Phase 5 QA gate, these deliverables must follow a consistent format, making this a team-wide baseline skill.

### Round 2

**Strategy Analyst** (Round 2):
Building on Round 1: maintaining `competitive-intelligence` and `financial-modeling`, but the discussion revealed a need for a more integrated `market-entry-analysis` approach. Rather than a separate skill, this should be a module within `competitive-intelligence`. New proposal: `insight-synthesis`. The process of combining outputs from multiple agents (competitive research, organizational diagnostics, technical assessment) into one unified strategic insight is not currently standardized. This skill would be used primarily by Strategy Analyst but also by Engagement Leader at the Phase 2 approval gate.

**Change Management Partner** (Round 2):
`insight-synthesis` is important — when integrating multiple diagnostic results, omitting organizational context frequently makes strategies unexecutable. This skill must include a cultural filtering step. Maintaining `stakeholder-alignment` and `org-readiness-assessment`. New proposal: `change-impact-assessment`. A standard framework for systematically mapping how a given change affects specific organizational layers, processes, and roles. Without it, change management plans rely on intuition. The outputs of this skill feed directly into Communications Lead's stakeholder-specific message strategy.

**Communications Lead** (Round 2):
When `change-impact-assessment` outputs arrive together with `stakeholder-alignment` results, the inputs for `executive-presentation` are complete — this flow will become the core Phase 3 pipeline. Confirming `narrative-framework` and `executive-presentation`. New proposal: `consulting-report-writing`. McKinsey/BCG-style consulting reports follow a unique format distinct from academic papers or general business documents: issue tree structure, MECE principle application, recommendation impact-feasibility matrices. Standardizing this as a skill guarantees a quality floor for all deliverables.

**Solutions Architect** (Round 2):
Confirming `solution-design` with dependency and risk sections included per Delivery Manager's request. New proposal: `technical-feasibility`. A standard framework for evaluating whether a proposed solution is actually implementable — technology maturity, integration complexity, data migration risk, operational capability gaps. This skill is needed both in Phase 1 when Strategy Analyst conducts market analysis and in Phase 3 when designing solutions. Combined with Delivery Manager's `project-delivery` skill, this completes the "design feasibility → execution feasibility" validation chain.

**Delivery Manager** (Round 2):
Confirming `project-delivery`. The outputs of `technical-feasibility` become a primary input for the risk register, so the interface between the two skills must be clearly defined. Final proposal: `stakeholder-review-management`. Feedback loop management in consulting accounts for 30-40% of total schedule. Standardizing reviewer selection, feedback collection structure, conflict resolution, and change history management will significantly improve Phase 4 delivery quality and speed. This creates a structure where Communications Lead's `consulting-report-writing` outputs feed directly into the review cycle.

## Synthesis

**Points of Agreement:**
1. `narrative-framework` to be shared between Communications Lead and Solutions Architect
2. `solution-design` to include dependency and risk sections (Delivery Manager requirement)
3. `insight-synthesis` to include cultural filtering step (Change Management Partner requirement)
4. `competitive-intelligence` to include market entry analysis module

**Open Issues:**
- Interface boundary between `financial-modeling` and `technical-feasibility` — where the business case ends and technical evaluation begins needs clarification during skill authoring
